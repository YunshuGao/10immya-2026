📖 Quick Reference

Look things up fast — UI layout, shortcuts, common tasks, and a glossary.

Adobe Express — main editor layout

Top toolbar → sharing & download. Left panel → assets to add. Right panel → edit whatever you selected. Bottom → pages (or timeline for video).

Video editor — timeline view

Preview on the left. Properties of the selected clip on the right. Timeline at the bottom — drag yellow handles to trim, click between clips to add transitions. Music and captions sit on their own tracks underneath.

Glossary

The words you'll hear all unit. Come back here when one trips you up.

Aspect ratio
The shape of your video (square 1:1, vertical 9:16, horizontal 16:9).
Asset
Any element you add — image, video clip, text, shape, audio.
Auto-caption
Adobe Express AI that listens to your video and generates captions. Always check accuracy.
B-roll
Supporting footage (not the main subject speaking) that covers edits and extends narrative.
Canvas
The main working area where you design — the 'page' you see in the editor.
Clip
A single piece of video footage on the timeline.
Crop
Cut away edges of a photo/video to change framing.
Duplicate
Make a copy of a layer, page, or clip. Shortcut: Ctrl+D / Cmd+D.
Export
Save your finished project as a file (PNG, JPG, PDF, MP4).
Frame rate (fps)
Frames per second. 30fps = standard, 60fps = smoother motion.
Hook
The first 3 seconds of your video that earns the next 57.
Layer
An individual element stacked on your canvas (text, image, shape).
Mood board
A visual collection of images and words capturing the vibe of your project.
MP4
The video file format you export — works everywhere.
Page
In multi-page docs, each page is a design frame. In video, the 'scene' concept.
Remix
Duplicate a template or project and modify it.
Resolution
Pixel count — 1080p = 1920×1080 = standard. 4K = higher detail.
Rough cut
First-pass edit: clips in order, no polish. Structure first, polish later.
Royalty-free
Music / images you can use without paying. Adobe Stock has a big free library.
Shot list
A pre-production list of every shot you plan to film — type, angle, movement.
Stock
Free images, videos, music from Adobe's library. Search in the left panel.
Storyboard
Visual sequence of your video shot-by-shot, usually 6–12 frames.
Template
A pre-designed project you remix with your own content.
Timeline
Horizontal bar at the bottom of the video editor where clips live.
Transition
Effect between two clips. 90% of the time: use 'hard cut' (no transition) or 'Fade'.
Trim
Shorten a clip by dragging its start/end handles.

✅ What you'll hand in
A Genre Mood Board PNG uploaded to Classroom.

Check yourself

What's the purpose of a mood board (in 1 sentence)?
To capture the feeling / vibe of your project visually before you start making decisions about specific scenes.
How do you add a stock image to your canvas?
Click 'Photos' in the left panel → type a search word → drag the image onto the canvas.

Your 8-Frame Storyboard (Task 2a MAIN WORK)

L06 ⏱ 45 min

The big one. Today you build the storyboard that'll guide your entire video.

🎯 Goal
A complete 8-frame storyboard in Adobe Express, ready for submission.

Steps

  1. 1Open your chosen template from MP05.
  2. 2Rename the project: 'T2a Storyboard — [Your Name]'.
  3. 3For EACH of the 8 frames:
  4. 4 • Add a simple sketch or placeholder image (stock photo is fine).
  5. 5 • Add a caption in the frame: 'Scene X — description'.
  6. 6 • Add timing below: 'Duration: Xs'.
  7. 7Ensure the TOTAL timing adds up to 60–90 seconds.
  8. 8Check narrative flow: does frame 1 hook? Does frame 8 close? Does the middle make sense?
  9. 9Save + share link in Google Classroom for teacher feedback.
💡 Pro tip
Don't get fancy on the visuals. A stick-figure storyboard that tells a clear story beats a pretty one that confuses.
🧭 If you're stuck
Got fewer than 8 ideas? That's a sign your script/topic needs more depth. Add B-roll moments (hands, objects, transitions) to fill frames.
✅ What you'll hand in
Complete 8-frame storyboard shared via Adobe Express link in Classroom.

Check yourself

How many seconds should your 8-frame storyboard total?
60–90 seconds. Add up each frame's duration.
What's more important: visually pretty frames OR clear narrative?
Clear narrative. Stick figures are fine — your storyboard exists to communicate the story, not to win an art prize.

Before/After Lighting Demo

L12 ⏱ 20 min

Show the POWER of good lighting with your own mini-demo.

🎯 Goal
A 10-second video showing the same subject in bad lighting then good lighting.

Steps

  1. 1Record a friend (or yourself) in BAD lighting (back to window, overhead fluorescent, etc.) — 5 seconds.
  2. 2Change the lighting: face towards window OR add a lamp. Record again — 5 seconds.
  3. 3Create new Adobe Express Video → drag both clips to timeline.
  4. 4Add text overlay: 'BEFORE' on clip 1, 'AFTER' on clip 2.
  5. 5Add a snappy transition between them.
  6. 6Export MP4. Upload to Classroom.
💡 Pro tip
Lighting changes feel tiny but on camera they're enormous. Your mates will go 'whoa that's a huge difference'.

✅ What you'll hand in
10-second before/after lighting demo MP4 uploaded to Classroom.

Check yourself

What's the single biggest lighting change you can make with zero budget?
Face TOWARDS a natural light source (window) instead of away from it.
Why put 'BEFORE' and 'AFTER' text overlays on your demo?
So viewers instantly understand the comparison without needing you to explain it.

MP15

Cut Order Experiment

L15 ⏱ 20 min

Editing is storytelling. Today you try TWO different orders for the same footage and see how they feel.

🎯 Goal
Two versions of a rough sequence (Order A vs Order B) to compare.

Steps

  1. 1Open your Task 2b Adobe Express project.
  2. 2Duplicate the page (bottom of screen → Pages → Duplicate).
  3. 3ORDER A: arrange clips chronologically (the order they happened).
  4. 4ORDER B (on the duplicated page): arrange clips for IMPACT — strongest clip first, reveal/twist last.
  5. 5Preview each. Write down which feels stronger and why.
  6. 6Keep the winning order. Delete or archive the other page.
  7. 7Save your project.
💡 Pro tip
The order you FILMED clips is almost never the order you should USE them. Editing breaks chronology on purpose.
🧭 If you're stuck
Both orders feel the same? Try a wilder Order C — put the ending first. Most editing breakthroughs come from 'what if I just tried...'
✅ What you'll hand in
Decision noted in your project: 'Final order = A / B / other', one sentence rationale.

Check yourself

Why try TWO different cut orders for your footage?
To feel how editing reshapes meaning. The filmed order is almost never the strongest told order.
In Order B (impact order), what goes first?
Your strongest / most hooking clip — not the chronological first moment.

Build the skeleton of your final video. Don't polish. Just get clips in order.

Steps

MP17

Transition Pacing Pass

L17 ⏱ 25 min

Transitions should feel INVISIBLE. Today we apply them with intent.

🎯 Goal
Your rough cut refined with purposeful transitions.

Steps

  1. 1Open your rough cut.
  2. 2Identify the TWO transition points that matter most (usually story beats).
  3. 3Apply a transition there — usually 'Fade' or 'Cross dissolve' is safest.
  4. 4Everywhere else: use hard cuts (no transition). Hard cuts are a choice, not laziness.
  5. 5NO ZOOM transitions. NO spin transitions. NO glitch. (Trust me.)
  6. 6Preview at full screen — do the transitions serve the story or distract?
  7. 7Remove any that feel gimmicky.
  8. 8Save + export preview. Upload to Classroom.
💡 Pro tip
Amateur = lots of transitions. Pro = mostly hard cuts, with 1–2 well-placed soft transitions for emphasis.
🧭 If you're stuck
Want to use a fancy transition? Ask yourself: 'Would this be in a real ad/film?' If no, kill it.

Refined video v2 MP4 uploaded to Classroom.

No. Hard cuts + the occasional 'Fade' or 'Cross dissolve' beat fancy transitions every time in short-form.

Accessibility Caption Pass

L19 ⏱ 20 min

Captions aren't optional. They make your video watchable for 15% of viewers with hearing loss AND for the 80%+ who watch with sound off.

🎯 Goal
Your video with accurate, well-timed captions across every spoken moment.

Steps

  1. 1Open your project.

  3. 3CRITICAL: review EVERY caption. Auto-captions make errors, especially with names / slang / Australian accents.
  4. 4Fix timing — captions should appear slightly BEFORE the word is spoken, disappear slightly AFTER.
  5. 5Styling: white text, black background box, bottom-third of screen. Readable on phone.
  6. 6Export final version.
  7. 7Upload MP4 + a note confirming: 'I checked every caption. Found X errors and fixed them.'
